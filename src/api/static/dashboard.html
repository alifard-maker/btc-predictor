Guide · dashboard, bots, buttons & terminology

This dashboard runs server-side bots (paper or live on Kalshi) plus model forecasts. All times are Eastern (ET). Expand sections below for each area.

Top of page — header & global actions

Refresh
Reloads the active tab (predictions, bot status, health). Auto-runs every ~10s.
Sign out
Ends your session (shared password gate).
Reset (zoom)
Resets page zoom to 100%, not bot data.
Kalshi cash
Your linked account balance (live trading). Bot sizing still uses max at-risk $/hour or $/slot, not your full wallet.
Fresh start all bots (footer of status strip)
Clears every bot trade log and resets each paper bankroll. Live bots: log wipe only — open Kalshi positions are unchanged. Use after a code release for clean stats.

Paper bot performance · calibration report

Collapsed panel under the header. Shows closed round-trip stats from bot SQLite logs — not the same as hourly model calibration further down each tab.

  • Scoped to your tab — ETH Hourly shows ETH bots only; BTC Hourly shows BTC; 15m tabs show that asset’s slot bot.
  • Experiment table — P&L since experiment_start_at in config (current code era). Main hourly + trial bots side by side.
  • Rolling windows (1h–48h) — Exits in that time window from the trade log. After a fresh start, these stay near zero.
  • 60d / per-bot blocks — Entry-price buckets, spread buckets, auto-tune notes. Trials are labeled and excluded from “main” totals.
  • Ask-edge gate — Bot only enters when model probability beats Kalshi ask by N cents after fees.

Bot card — controls on every auto-bet panel

Auto-bet
Master ON/OFF. When ON, the server polls Kalshi every ~5–10s and enters/exits without the browser open.
Paper / Live
Paper = simulated fills and bankroll. Live = real Kalshi orders (requires API keys + live password prompt).
Max at-risk $/hour (hourly) or $/slot (15m)
Cap on concurrent exposure and (usually) total $ entered per hour/slot. Auto-refill (live): winning streak extends effective cap; paper: refills bankroll when empty.
STRONG / ACTIONABLE
Filter entries. Both OFF = “free mode” (any actionable signal; regime advisory on hourly).
Use profits
Live: add realized hour P&L to deployable budget (separate from auto-refill win boost).
Fresh start (paper)
Clears trade log, open positions in DB, cooldowns, tuning state; resets paper bankroll to max cap. Settings (mode, caps) kept.
Clear history (live)
Same log wipe as fresh start for live — does not close Kalshi positions. Use when dashboard log is stale vs exchange.
Reset bankroll (paper)
Resets paper cash to max cap without deleting trade history (rare; prefer fresh start).
Override daily cap
One-click lift of the $50/day loss stop for this bot (resets at midnight NY).
Interval record (live/paper)
Win/loss/breakeven per hour or slot from closed P&L. Counts only trades since last Fresh start or Clear history. Breakeven = net P&L within ±0.5¢ for that hour. Live: Kalshi sync no longer backfills hours before fresh start.
Sync fills (live)
Pull recent Kalshi fills into the log; fixes reconcile drift. Respects fresh-start cutoff — won’t re-import old hours as breakevens.

Hourly predictions — what you’re looking at

  • Settlement range — Model band where the index (BRTI / ERTI) is expected at the hour close (μ ± scaled σ).
  • Forecast μ (mu) — Blended expected settle price; drives which strikes/bands are shown.
  • Most likely band — Highest model probability among range bands near μ (not necessarily a BUY signal).
  • Best edge — Largest |model − Kalshi| gap among nearby contracts.
  • Hour open @ :00 — Snapshot at top of hour; frozen preview until :05 official lock.
  • Locked @ :05 — Official snapshot for calibration; does not move when the market drifts.
  • Hourly calibration (per tab) — After settle, compares locked picks to actual index (Brier, BUY hit rate). Different from the top paper bot performance panel.

Contract signals (BTC & ETH hourly tables)

Each row is one Kalshi contract. The signal is which side to buy, not a vague “lean” toward a price level.

BUY YES
Buy the Yes leg — model says Kalshi underprices this outcome vs model probability (positive edge on Yes).
BUY NO
Buy the No leg — model says Kalshi overprices Yes. On a range band, this means you do not expect settle inside that exact $20 bucket — even if it sits next to the most-likely band.
VALUE YES (thresholds only)
Cheap OTM Yes tail — model <50% but Kalshi is even cheaper; speculative Yes, not a high-confidence direction call.
FADE YES (thresholds only)
Kalshi Yes looks rich vs model on a likely leg — fade = Buy NO on that contract (not Buy Yes). Dashboard shows Buy NO (fade).
NEUTRAL
Edge below minimum or no Kalshi mid — no actionable mispricing tag. Far OTM rows may also show low predictability or priced in · no edge.

Strategy 2 (range bands) — common confusion

Every band row asks: “Does the index settle inside this band?” Bands are mutually exclusive. A strong peak at $1,550–$1,569 does not make neighboring bands BUY YES — if model gives ~1% in a side bucket but Kalshi prices ~10%, the tag is BUY NO on that bucket.

15m tab — extra signals

LONG / SHORT / NO TRADE
Directional bet on the whole slot vs t=0 (UP probability thresholds ~57% / ~43%).
2nd Chance
Reassessment ~4 minutes into the slot using fresher features.
LATE LONG / LATE SHORT
Conservative mid-slot entry when move, time on side of ref, and momentum align.
FLIP LONG / FLIP SHORT
Exit a losing open bet and take the other side once per slot when reassessment is strong.
HOLD / TAKE PROFIT / CUT LOSS
Intra-slot exit guidance if you already have a position.

Calibration vocabulary

  • Brier score — Mean squared error of probability vs outcome (lower = better calibrated).
  • In-band hit rate — % of hours settle index landed inside the locked range band.
  • All BUY-signal bands @ lock — Every band with BUY YES or BUY NO frozen in the near-μ slice at :05 (can be multiple per hour).
  • Epoch / all-time — Current scoring window vs archived history (15m tab); hourly uses resolved event rows since last reset.

Performance & errors

  • Health: Timeout after …ms — Server busy (often Kalshi API or SQLite lock). Wait and hit Refresh; panels load independently.
  • Loading… on a panel — That API is still running or failed silently; check the red error strip or Refresh.
  • 429 / circuit breaker — Kalshi rate limit; bot entries pause briefly; banner may show at top.
Guidance only — not financial advice. Kalshi settlement uses CF Benchmarks indices (BRTI / ERTI). Past calibration does not guarantee future results.

How to read this dashboard

This page helps you decide whether BTC finishes above or below its price at the start of each 15-minute Eastern Time slot (:00, :15, :30, :45). It does not place trades. Refresh is automatic every 5 seconds.

Your price at t=0 (top box)

t=0 is the Kalshi KXBTC15M Target price — the BRTI 60-second average in the minute before slot open. Now updates every second from live CF Benchmarks BRTI (requires KALSHI_PRIVATE_KEY on Railway). Without it, Coinbase is used as an approximate live feed; P&L is always vs Kalshi t=0.

Current 15-minute slot

  • Reference (t=0) — Starting price for this slot. UP means close above this; DOWN means close below.
  • UP / DOWN % — Model estimate for where price lands at slot close vs t=0.
  • LONG / SHORT / NO TRADE — LONG if UP ≥ ~57%; SHORT if UP ≤ ~43%; otherwise sit out.
  • Expected move — Rough $ move implied by recent volatility (not a target).
  • Confidence — How far the model is from 50/50 (higher = more decisive).
  • Live now — Live CF Benchmarks BRTI (Kalshi passthrough).
  • Kalshi YES mid — Market-implied UP probability on KXBTC15M (cents).

Signal breakdown

Ingredients behind the UP/DOWN bars — not separate trade signals. Green bar = leans UP for the next slot; red = leans DOWN; length = strength.

Short-term (1h)
1h momentum, slot return, and how many of the last four 15m bars closed up.
Medium (4h)
Four-hour trend on 15m candles.
Regime (12h)
Twelve-hour move; extended moves may be faded (mean-reversion nudge).
Context
Volume vs 4h average, RSI (14), and distance from 12h VWAP.

System status

Health of the API, scheduler, and Kalshi BRTI feed. Green dots = OK.

Live position · intra-slot exit

If you bet at open, use HOLD / TAKE PROFIT / CUT LOSS. If the open was NO TRADE, the monitor watches the slot and may suggest a LATE LONG/SHORT only after several minutes of consistent price action (not instant flips).

WATCH
No bet at open — collecting slot history; shows outlook after ~2 min.
LATE LONG / LATE SHORT
Conservative mid-slot entry when move vs t=0, time on side of ref, and momentum align (needs ~4+ min elapsed, 3+ min left).
HOLD
Position still on track; no urgent action.
TAKE PROFIT
In profit and move may be exhausted — consider locking gains.
CUT LOSS
Losing with momentum against you — consider exiting; includes reassessed UP/DOWN for time left in the slot.
FLIP LONG / FLIP SHORT
Open bet losing past stop and reassessment strongly favors the other side — exit open leg, bet opposite. One flip per slot; after that only HOLD/TP/CUT on the flipped side.
  • Ref (t=0) — Kalshi BRTI target at slot open.
  • Now — Live BRTI index.
  • Move vs t=0 — Dollar difference between live price and t=0 (e.g. +$50 if BTC is $50 above the slot open).
  • Time left — Countdown to slot close.

Calibration

Three Brier scores: slot-open (prob at t=0), 2nd Chance (reassessment at t+4min), late entry (first LATE signal), and flip (after losing open). Lower is better. All-time stats persist across epoch resets.

Prediction history

Recent slots: reference at t=0, UP probability, signal, and actual outcome once the slot resolves.
